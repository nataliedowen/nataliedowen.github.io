Target Audience

The target audience for Fast Cast Weather is the residents of the 3 locations for which the site will report the forecast, as well as those traveling to those locations. The intended gender is both male and female, and the age range is 16-65 years old. Those under 16 will likely depend on their parents for weather information, and those above 65 generally rely on more traditional forms of weather forcast information. The will access the site from their smart phone, tablet, or computer.

Persona 1

Jenny, an 18 year old female, lives in Preston, Idaho. She graduated from high school last year. Jenny loves the outdoors, and is always out doing something. She curernlty lives at home with her parents, but will start school next year at BYU-I.

Scenario 1

Jenny wants to go camping with her friends. They will be gone gto about 3 days, and shes not sure what the weather will be like. After all, she can't pack for the weather if she deosn't know it. She decides to go to fastcastweather.com and check the 5 day forecast feature.

Scenario 2

Jenny needs to drive to work, but is worried that it will snow hwile she's working and that she won't be able to get back in her little Honda Civic Hybrid. If it will snow, she needs her dad to take her to work in his truck, but she only has 5 minutes before he will be out the door. Quickly, she checks the forecast for the day on fastcast.com

Scenario 3

Jenny has a friend who is coming into town, and called Jenny to ask that the weather will be like, trying to decide which coat to pack. Jenny sends her the link to fastcastweather.com so that she can pack accordingly.

Scenario 4

Jenny has a friend in Fish Haven who swears that it's snowing ther right now. Jenny can't believe it, and says there is no way since it's not snowing in Preston! Jenny heads to fastcastweather.com to check the current weather in Fish Haven to see if it really is snowing.

Scenario 5

Jenny was talking to a friend from out of town who asked her what idaho looks like. Excited, jenny sends her to fastcastweather.com so that she can check out the gallery feature and see what Idaho looks like!
